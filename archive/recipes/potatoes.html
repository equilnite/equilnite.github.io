Potatoes

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Various seasonings (oregano, salt, garlic powder, etc...)
  • Cooking oil
  • A nice cut of meat with fat (start with slabs—like a steak—of meat)

Cookingware

  • Two pans (for efficiency)
  • Knife
  • Cutting board
  • Spatula / similar thing for flipping

Steps

  1. Start by cubing potatoes into thumbsized pieces (make sure to cut out any imperfections in the potatoes)
  2. Heat oil in two pans (more oil in one than the other)
  3. Add the potatoes into the pan with more oil, immediately flip after ~20 seconds. While you're at it, sear the cut of meat in the other pan for 30 seconds on each side (if it's beef) or cook all the way through otherwise. Take out the meat and put it on the cutting board when done.
  4. Make sure you are still taking care of your potatoes; you want them to brown, so don't flip them too frequently and don't let them burn. You can add your seasonings when a majority of the potatoes have browning.
  5. Cut the meat into thumb sized pieces.
  6. When your potatoes are just about done, you can add the meat into the potatoes (and add the cooking oil from the meat if you want too). If you are using beef, you might want to cook just a little longer to get rid of some of the rareness.
